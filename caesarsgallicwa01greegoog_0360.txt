Caesar weighed anchor at sunset (about 8 nm). A gentle breeze was
then blowing from the S-W.; but at the same time a tidal current was

died down, and the current also began to set in the opposite direction
(NME) With this current the Romans drined, and at dawn the British
coast appeared on their left. They had passed by (relictam) the North
Foreland, aud were drifting sarther away from the land. But at about
this time (5 mm.) the tide changed, and the crurent again began to flow
towards the S.W. With its help, and that of the oars, the fleet now
began to move towards the land, aiming to reach the same point that had
been found in the previous expedition to be the most convenient for
debarking. Here they arrived at about noon (Gdler).
